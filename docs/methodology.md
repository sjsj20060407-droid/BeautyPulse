# Methodology

## Research questions

1. How do beauty market groups differ in content volume and engagement distribution?
2. Do platform results change after controlling for product category?
3. Which selling points and pain points appear most frequently for lip and base makeup?
4. Does a product discovered through an Olive Young trend source also receive attention on Chinese content platforms?

## Sampling

- Use Olive Young's official bestseller page only to build a K-beauty candidate list.
- Select four brands per market group after a small pilot search.
- Keep lip and base makeup as the main comparable categories.
- For each brand-platform pair, target 30-50 public posts.
- Sample both relevance and recent-sort results when the platform interface permits it.
- Record the collection timestamp and cap comments per post.
- Do not interpret search results as a representative sample of all platform users.

## Measurement caveats

- Platform recommendation and search algorithms affect visibility.
- Engagement is cumulative and older posts have more exposure time.
- Brand-country labels can be ambiguous after acquisitions or joint ventures.
- Sponsored-content detection is a disclosure-keyword hint, not ground truth.
- Lexicon sentiment misses sarcasm, mixed opinions and context.
- Cross-platform counts are not directly comparable without normalization.

## Analysis strategy

- Report medians and distributions instead of relying only on means.
- Compare within the same product category.
- Use within-platform/category percentiles for high-engagement labels.
- Show both raw metrics and the weighted exploratory score.
- Separate descriptive findings from causal claims.
